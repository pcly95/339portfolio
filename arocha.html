A web account tool that helps volunteer candidates through a streamlined registration and sign-up process. This tool helps them consistently seek volunteer opportunities, allowing for growth in the company’s global community.

  • Roles: UX Research, Interactions, Project Planning
  • Tools: Sketch, Flinto, Adobe Illustrator
  • Year: 2017
  • Context: Case Study (3 months)
  • Team: Aron Chen, Gabriel Yeung

research + process methods

The focus for this project was to experiment with various research methods to understand our participant group at a human-experience level: their motivations, struggles, values, and aspirations. Below are several select methods we found to be most useful to relate to their needs.

ethnography study + interviews

The purpose of the ethnography study was to get a better understanding of the company’s ethos. We conducted 4 in-person interviews with various expertises to learn how each group comes together to work as a whole.

persona:

The personas were built off of the people we interviewed. It served as a useful tool that helped guide the rest of our design process.

participatory workshop

The participatory workshop was where we got a better understanding of the internal processes of the company. We were able to map out the current experience of their processes and see where we alleviate existing pain points.

customer journey framework

The journey framework was developed based on our understanding of the persona and their current internal processes. We mapped out the ideal experience for a volunteer who is interested in joining opportunities at A Rocha.

information architecture

Given the short span of 1 week to execute the prototype, I quickly mapped out the general information architecture of the web application in order to move into the execution phase. This map acted as the foundation for the prototype.

reflection

This project helped me realise the value in working directly with your target audience. Being able to gain direct feedback and suggestions and understanding the company as a whole gave me a newfound appreciation for user research.
